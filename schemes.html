Government Schemes for Loans

Explore subsidized and supportive schemes from government agencies. These can help with business, education, and personal financing. Always check eligibility and apply through official channels.

MSME Loans (Ministry of MSME)

Support for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises with low-interest loans, credit guarantees, and subsidies.

  • Eligibility: Businesses with turnover up to ₹250 crore.
  • Benefits: Interest subvention up to 2%, collateral-free loans up to ₹1 crore.
  • Link: Ministry of MSME

Student Credit Card Scheme (Banks like SBI)

Pre-approved credit cards for students with benefits like rewards on education expenses and no annual fees.

  • Eligibility: Students aged 18-25, enrolled in higher education.
  • Benefits: Credit limit up to ₹1 lakh, waiver on late fees, rewards on spends.
  • Link: SBI Student Credit Card

Education Loans (Central Scheme)

Loans for higher education in India or abroad, with subsidized rates and moratorium periods.

  • Eligibility: Students admitted to recognized courses, family income up to ₹4.5 lakh.
  • Benefits: Interest rate 1% above base rate, loan up to ₹20 lakh, repayment after course completion.
  • Link: UGC Guidelines or Canara Bank

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)

Loans for non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises to encourage entrepreneurship.

  • Eligibility: Individuals or businesses in manufacturing, trading, services.
  • Benefits: Loans from ₹50,000 to ₹10 lakh, no collateral for loans up to ₹5 lakh.
  • Link: Mudra Yojana

Stand-Up India Scheme

Loans for SC/ST and women entrepreneurs to start businesses in manufacturing, services, or trading.

  • Eligibility: SC/ST or women, project cost ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore.
  • Benefits: Composite loans, interest subvention, credit guarantee.
  • Link: Stand-Up India
